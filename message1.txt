SUBJECT: Appeal to the UK Parliament: support for the Iranian people, Iran’s territorial integrity, and the role of Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi

BODY:
Honourable Representatives of the UK Parliament,

We write to you as Iranian citizens and supporters of human rights, residing in the United Kingdom and across Europe, to draw your attention to the situation of the Iranian people and to the importance of a clear and consistent position by the United Kingdom.

The Iranian people deserve freedom, dignity, and full recognition of their fundamental rights. For nearly half a century, they have lived under the repression of the Islamic Republic, a system that has deprived millions of citizens of their most basic freedoms. At this historic moment, it is essential that the United Kingdom firmly and unequivocally support the Iranian people in their struggle to bring this regime to an end.

Millions of Iranians, both inside and outside the country, are calling for the end of the Islamic Republic and for the construction of a free, democratic Iran that respects human rights. In this historic process, many Iranians recognise Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi as the only leader with the legitimacy and credibility necessary to represent the will of the Iranian people. His role in guiding a peaceful transition from the Islamic Republic toward a democratic system, legitimately chosen by the people, is regarded by the vast majority of Iranians as fundamentally important.

We ask that:

• The United Kingdom continue to support the Iranian people politically and morally in their aspiration for freedom and for the end of the Islamic Republic.
• The inviolability of Iran’s territorial integrity be reaffirmed in all national and international forums.
• Strong support be given to the legitimate will of the Iranian people to determine the future institutional and political structure of their country.
• The fundamental role of Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi be recognised as a central figure and qualified leader to guide the peaceful transition from the Islamic Republic toward a democratic system chosen by the Iranian people.
• Maximum protection of civilians be supported in every institutional forum, and that the protection of the population not involved in military activities be treated as an absolute priority.

We make this appeal in respect for and in shared commitment to the democratic values that guide the United Kingdom, and in the conviction that freedom, self-determination, and the protection of civilians are universally recognised principles that must be defended in every place and official context.

Respectfully,
